Hi, my name is Lizzie and I am currently a Postdoctoral fellow in the Climate and Fluid Physics Group at the Australian National University funded by the Australian Reserach Council Centre of Excellence in Climate Extremes .

I am oceanographer with in interest in understanding how ocean physics impacts biogeochemical cycles in the ocean, including air sea carbon exchange and phytoplankton productivity. I am currently studying the role of mesoscale and submesocale features in the Southern Ocean on biogeochemistry using high resolution global ocean models.

I completed a PhD at Imperial College London working with Dr Ali Mashayek within the within the MODEL group, affiliatied with the Grantham Institute’s Science and Solutions for a Changing Planet Doctoral Training Partnership and funded by the Centre for Doctoral Training in Civil and Sustainable Engineering .

My research focussed on the role of small-scale vertical turbulent mixing in the Southern Ocean on biogeochemical cycles.

Prior to completing my PhD, I obtained a M.Sc in Oceanography from the National Oceanography Centre at the University of Southampton and a B.Sc in Natural Sciences from Durham University.
